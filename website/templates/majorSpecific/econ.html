Economics Roadmap

This first-pass Econ roadmap is based on Colby's major requirements and is meant to help students think about the order and timing of the major. The main idea is to finish the early core soon enough to leave room for upper-level electives, EC325, and a senior seminar.

Selected year: Not chosen
Colby's Economics major requires EC133, EC134, EC223, EC224, EC225, and EC325, plus one senior seminar, three additional electives at 211 or above, at least two of those at the 300 level, and Math 120 or higher.

Core Courses to Plan Around

  • EC133
  • EC134
  • Math 120 or higher
  • EC223
  • EC224
  • EC225
  • EC325
  • One senior seminar
  • Three electives at 211 or above
  • At least two 300-level electives

Experiences to Look For

  • Research with economics faculty
  • Internships in finance, consulting, government, or policy
  • Career center workshops and recruiting events
  • Alumni networking
  • Exploring the financial markets concentration if relevant

Planning Notes

  • Finish EC133 and math early if possible
  • EC223 requires EC133 and calculus with at least a C-
  • Keep track of grades needed to continue in the major
  • Senior seminars must be completed at Colby
  • If you may want grad school, consider more math and possibly CS151

Freshman / Sophomore Focus

Use the first half of college to make progress on EC133, EC134, and the math requirement. The earlier you finish the intro work, the easier it is to fit EC223, EC224, EC225, and later electives into a manageable schedule.

Junior Focus

Junior year is a good time to be deep into the upper-level sequence, start building toward 300-level electives, and connect coursework to internships, research, or other career-related experience.

Senior Focus

Senior year should be about finishing EC325, completing the seminar requirement, and using electives to sharpen a specific interest area. Students interested in honors or a thesis should plan ahead and talk with the department early.
