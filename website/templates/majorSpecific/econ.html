Economics Roadmap

This Econ roadmap is organized around a Colby student's path through the major. It highlights what classes to prioritize each year, plus useful programs and resources for finance, consulting, policy, and quant-oriented students.

Selected year: Not chosen
Main idea: finish the intro and methods work early enough that junior and senior year can be used for upper-level electives, econometrics, and seminar work.

Classes by Year

Freshman Year

  • EC 133: Microeconomics
  • EC 134: Macroeconomics

Sophomore Year

  • EC 224: Macro Theory
  • EC 223: Micro Theory
  • PS 214: Research Methods or EC 293: Econ Research Methods
  • Any Math class between MA 125 to MA 160

Junior Year

  • Econ elective above 200
  • EC 393: Econometrics

Senior Year

  • Econ elective at the 300 level
  • Econ seminar

How to Use These

These are not all required. They are examples of the kinds of programs students can look into depending on their interests. Freshman and sophomore programs are useful for getting exposure early, while junior summer is usually the most important internship summer.

Planning Notes

  • Use freshman and sophomore year to get the core sequence moving early.
  • Methods and math work matter because they open up upper-level econ coursework.
  • Junior year is a good time to connect classes with internships and recruiting.
  • Senior year should focus on finishing upper-level work and seminar requirements well.
